Researcher / Designer / Developer / Teacher

I am an accidental designer and hobbyist software developer. I am a design researcher by training and a data visualisation practitioner by passion.

For a majority period, I am in the academia—at IIT Bombay‐Monash Research Academy, Mumbai—doing research, from where I am completing Ph.D. in Data Visualisation,—at IIIT-Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India—I obtained a M.Tech in Computer Science & Engineering (2017), and —at Central University of Jharkhand, Ranchi, India—I obtained an Integrated M.Sc. in Applied Mathematics (2014).

I am building Beyond Ph.D.: a platform for aspiring and current Ph.D. students to get their general queries answered, Lizzie Labs: a venture to boost the employability of India's next generation through the use of AI as a smart assistant, and Lizzie Care Foundation: a think tank for Universal Access to Education.

I am an optimist, and hopeful of the future generation. I believe with the right motivation, we can make the world a better place.

I discovered the idea of code sometime in 2008 after getting a personal computer. Starting with C, I have been going down the rabbithole ever since. These days, I mostly write programs in Javascript.

This website first went online in 2020 and has since undergone numerous iterations. It has lived long on github site before getting a domain name.

The period brings back nostalgia—the discovery of code, the excitement of connecting to the world over dialup Internet, the gratification of relentless experimentation by trial and error.

That said, I am figuring out ways to combine my passion for design & code and tell stories to the world. I am a Nobody with a keen desire to keep learning throughout my life.
